Instructions

  • The robot is in a living room with three pertinent locations - The Couch, The Dining Table, and The Kitchen Counter.
  • There are three pertinent objects in this environment - The Jug, The Bowl, and The Cup (shown below)
  • The robot has been given the goal of grabbin the cup from the Kitchen Counter and taking it to the Couch (in anticipation of a human arriving to receive the cup).
  • If the robot has an error, it is designed to seek assistance from a remote human, such as yourself.
  • In the event of the error, your job is to direct the robot so that it ends up by the Couch with the Cup.

When assisting the robot, you will have four types of actions available to you:

Navigate to
The action type moves the robot base to the desired location (among the three available) in the room.
Look at
The robot moves away from its current location to point its camera at the desired location. After viewing that location for a few seconds, the robot returns to looking at its original location.
Update robot's location belief to
The robot maintains a belief of where in the room it is. If you think it is mistaken in its belief, you may update it by taking this action.
Pick up
If the robot recognizes an object and its gripper is empty, then it can be directed to pick up the object.
Stow object in gripper
If there is an object in the robot's gripper, it can be directed to stow that object on its base. This might involve background arm motion that is not visible in the robot's camera.
